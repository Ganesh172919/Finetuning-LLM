✨ Word Analogies in Vector Space

The most famous property of word embeddings: analogies work through vector arithmetic.

Watch the vector arithmetic play out in 3D
Why does this work? Word embeddings capture semantic relationships as directions in space. The "royalty" direction goes from man→king and woman→queen. The "gender" direction goes from king→queen and man→woman. These directions are consistent across many word pairs!

📐 Measuring Similarity

How do we measure how similar two words are? Cosine similarity — the angle between their vectors.

Drag vectors to change similarity
import numpy as np def cosine_similarity(a, b): """Compute cosine similarity between two vectors.""" return np.dot(a, b) / (np.linalg.norm(a) * np.linalg.norm(b)) # Example: word embeddings (simplified to 3D) king = np.array([0.8, 0.2, 0.9]) # royalty + male queen = np.array([0.8, 0.9, 0.9]) # royalty + female man = np.array([0.1, 0.2, 0.3]) # human + male woman = np.array([0.1, 0.9, 0.3]) # human + female # The famous analogy! result = king - man + woman print(f"king - man + woman = {result}") print(f"queen = {queen}") print(f"Similarity: {cosine_similarity(result, queen):.3f}")
